Pyrrhic

Pyrrhic is, Ole "HellBent" Madsen on bass, Øivind Vågane on drums, Sebastian Madsen and Roger Gregersen on Guitars and Emil Wynn as the vocalist.

A rocket launch into the metal scene of Agder is what we consider an apt, concise way to describe Pyrrhic. Of course the metal genre works a bit differently than the more mainstream genres, the way Pyrrhic went from nothing to hundres, then thousands and then ten thousands of listeners is impressive, and it makes sense. Take a listen.

Pyrrhic

Pyrrhic is a band that knew what they wanted to do and trusted in their vision from early in their career. They immediately started decorating their instruments like guitars and drum fonts on their drums early into their public careers with logo design from an artist friend, Pale Fiend. This is what you can do when your band manages to garner fans with just an EP released. They were with only this EP set to perform at the Southern Discomfort Metal Festival. This allowed them to gain even more exposure and since some of the band members have played concerts before, this let them really show off what they could do even though technically their band has just recently become official.



Pyrrhic being an ambitious and enthusiastic band, did not let this go to waste. After their song, EP "Their dilapidated hands" and a festival performance, they followed up with their song " Deafening The Light" released on major platforms like spotify and apple music and a new ep. "Vile Slumber". Now this EP didn't technically release immediately, however some more interested fans in the industry were allowed an early listen and with that, Pyrrhic got their first official review. occultblackmetalzine blessed Pyrrhic with an 8/10 rating on their second EP, even before release. Quite an achievement, hence why we thought it best to have that EP on the top of our page and of course, Pale Fiend came back to make the impressive artwork. Of course they came back to play at the Southern Discomfort Metal Festival, this time performing their EP Vile Slumber in it's entirety. Should not come as a surprise, such an impressive start and they're still going. Pyrrhic is a band to watch out for, truly.
